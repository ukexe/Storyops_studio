VIDEO BRIEF

Working title
How AI Is Changing Creative Work (And What You Should Do About It)

Purpose
Create a practical, optimistic explainer that helps small creative teams move
past the "AI replaces creativity" debate and identify useful workflow changes
they can make now.

Target audience
- Independent creators and two-to-ten-person content teams
- Agency producers, editors, and creative strategists
- Viewers who have experimented with generative AI but do not have a repeatable
  operating process

Audience insight
The audience is curious about AI but overwhelmed by new tools. They care more
about protecting quality, originality, and time than generating a higher volume
of generic content.

Primary objective
Show that the highest-value use of AI is coordinating creative work: turning
briefs into clear plans, checking consistency, and learning from performance.

Key messages
1. AI should remove coordination friction, not replace creative judgment.
2. Specialized agents can review briefs, scripts, and assets at different
   stages of production.
3. Teams should start with one measurable bottleneck and keep a human approval
   point in every workflow.

Tone and style
Confident, grounded, and creator-first. Avoid hype, fear, and unexplained
technical jargon. Use one concrete example from a YouTube production workflow.

Desired length
Approximately three minutes, with enough visual changes to support a brisk
explainer pace.

Required deliverables
- One 16:9 hero video
- One thumbnail
- Three short pull quotes for reuse

Constraints
- Do not claim that AI can predict virality.
- Do not show confidential client work or unlicensed brand assets.
- Keep the StoryOps Studio product mention educational rather than sales-led.

Success signal
Viewers should be able to name one creative-operations task they would delegate
to an AI agent while retaining final creative control.

Distribution plan
To be confirmed with the campaign owner.
